Ethan's Programing Journey

Humble beginings

I grew up in a small town called Scott City. There wasnt much to do there. I tried going to college, and quickly ran out of money. So I got a night shift job driving forklifts 60 plus hours a week. The long hours and never seeing the sun weighed me down. I changed jobs a couple of times. I eventually ended up working for a company called Silgan Plastics. They were building a new factory in Saint Louis. I was asked to help keep the old factory running, and if I did that there would be a full time job waiting for me there.

Fires of Industry

I moved with Silgan Plastics to Saint Louis. I quickly distinguished myself as one of the best operators in the factory. I was the guy that everyone came to with process related issues. Also I was the guy people came to when things caught fire, this was far too common on the factory floor. I was starting to wonder if the factory life was for me. I still wanted to go to college, but I didn't have enough time or money for that.

In my quest to find an affordable education I stumbled on to a tech school, Ranken Technical College. It was part time, industry trusted, and my employers would pay 75% of the cost. Wins all across the board as far as I was concerned. I picked the course "control systems technology." I made my first program in RSLogix5000. It was a program to control railroad crossing lights.

I learned a lot of things at Ranken. I wrote several programs for PLC's. I taught a Fanuc robot arm to undertake some rather complicated manuvers without crashing. I designed a human machine interface. My favorite parts of the course involved computers. I knew that I needed to get out of the factory, I just didn't yet know how.

Escape Attempt

One of the best things about Ranken were the job fairs. One Hundred plus companies all gather in one place to try to employ the newly trained talent. Yeah, I could have worked for Dr. Pepper or Budwiser but I was tired of the factory world. I found a company called AVI. They did audio visual installations. They worked in offices. I would have opportunities to program sound systems and displays. I cold called them until they hired me. They were impressed by my skills, and that I kept showing up.

I never really did get to program for them. I did learn quite a bit about Q-Sys, I had level one certification in a few months, but then the Corona Virus hit. I was laid off and that was the end of that career.

Self-study

I started working at a factory again. Aerospace. Depression. I had never really clearly defined my goals. Coming home covered in aluminum dust. I knew that I was stuck. I needed to have a new goal. I got one really important thing out of Ranken, I like computers!

Think about it. I could work from anywhere. I would make more money. I would come home everyday, If I even ever left home in the first place, CLEAN! Everyday!

So I started learning JavaScript on codecademy... and after several starts and stops it led me to Launchcode

Non-Stop

So I am here at launchcode. I didn't get enough stucture with codecademy. I am getting the much needed stucture, to pursue my goals, here. Every week is a rush, just trying to get everything done. I find myself singing along to that song from Alexander Hamilton: non-stop.

I admit though, I don't know where I am going with this class. I gotta pass, but what is next? I don't want to get just another job. I know also that that is probably were I will start. I gotta find something cool to do though. For now I am just look forward to having a career and the ability to buy a house.
